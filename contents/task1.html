Task 1 - Find out what a design system is.

A design system is a group of readily reusable components, guides, principles, tools, and patterns, ensuring that teams produce cohesive and consistent content efficiently. This collection of resources can contain building blocks of UI, guides on what content is suitable or required, colour palettes, typography, layout and even code snippets.

Colours describe certain actions or meaning such as red representing a need to be alert or danger, an example being a stop sign.

A relatable example of aesthetic principles given by design systems was provided by contentful.com in highway signage. Highway signage generally follows international conventions such as the Vienna Convention on Road Signs and Signals to achieve a degree of uniformity in traffic signage between countries and could be seen as a design system. A recogniseable example of this is the use of a variation of dark green or blue colours for the background color of highway signage with a thick border (usually white). Similarly, the font "Highway Gothic" is used consistently in many countries to "maximise legibility at distance and high speed".

However, design systems do not purely consist of guidelines and resources for aesthetic purposes but also content related guides as demonstrated by Shopify’s “Polaris” design system in how to write for a customer's understanding, “rather than defaulting to what corporations call things”.
