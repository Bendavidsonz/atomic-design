Personal Perspective of Atomic Design:

An atom in atomic design is defined as the smallest aspects components are made up of. For me, this makes sense to be resources such as typography, colours and shapes, which then means a button would be a molecule. Please note that atoms are different depending on the user/use case, and some people may decide both my view and Brad Frostâ€™s view are valid and have both colour, shape and a button as an atom. This however is counterintuitive to me.

Templates are reusable structural elements that provide a layout for content, pages are templates populated with content.

What does the brand do?

Material (Google):

Google is a multinational technology company one of the five big tech companies along with Amazon, Apple, Meta, and Microsoft. They focuse on a wide range of topics, from Artificial intelligence and cloud computing to computer software and consumer electronics, but are widely known for their prominent search engine.

Carbon (IBM):

IBM is the largest industrial research organization in the world, whilst also being one of the world's oldest and largest technology companies. IBM has been responsible for several technological innovations, including the automated teller machine (ATM), dynamic random-access memory (DRAM), the floppy disk, the hard disk drive, the magnetic stripe card, the relational database, the SQL programming language, and the UPC barcode.

Fluent (Microsoft):

Microsoft is a multinational and one of the world's leading technology corporations. They are renowned for their software products, services, and hardware devices such as the Windows operating system, productivity suites such as Microsofta Office, cloud computing services through Azure, and gaming platforms like Xbox.

Human Interface Guidelines (Apple):

Apple is a globally renowned technology company known mainly for the Macintosh computer, iPod, iPhone, iPad, and Apple Watch. It is also known for its well designed interface and graphical content, as well as the App Store, iCloud, Apple Music, and Apple TV+.

Does the system do a good job of assisting a designer to implement designs within the system?

I believe all the Design Systems I looked at assisted designers well. They had modular components, allowing for easy customization by the user. The depth and variety of their documentation allowed developers to be creative whilst maintaining an intuitive user interface, and each component came with documentation, tooltips and an example of its use case and scale.

I would say Apple's design system is more specific than the others, however is likely due to being used to develop for their own products, not a large range of different brands running different software etc.
