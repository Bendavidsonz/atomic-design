Atom + Molecule:

Shapes and Buttons are both displayed through the rounded button, which follows Material Designs baseline shapes guidelines. Material Design's guidelines state that a fully curved shape (circle) should be used for "Badge, Buttons, Icon buttons, Sliders, Switches and Search bar" and therefore is used correctly and effectively implemented

Organism:

Material Design describes the purpose of menus as a way to temporarily display a list of choices upon interaction. This interaction is typically initiated by the user through a button or icon. In the context of the Google Clock app, the Menu element follows these guidelines closely, serving as a means to provide users with access to various options and functionalities in a temporary and contextual manner.

Template/Page:

Navigation is displayed both as a template and as a page in Material Designs documentation. It is displayed as a template through the use of the navigation component built into Material design and as a page by filling the navigation bar with content (Buttons + Text). The Google Clock app implements a navigation bar and follows the guidelines of:

  • Provide access to three to five destinations
  • One destination is always active
  • Nav Bar positioned along the bottom of the screen

The navigation did not include icons as per the guidelines which I think would have been nice.
